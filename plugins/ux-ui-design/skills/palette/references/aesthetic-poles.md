# Aesthetic poles â€” the full catalog

Thirty-seven aesthetic poles organized by family. Each pole carries a one-line essence,
a starter palette (hex values), a starter type stack, distinctive component signatures,
best-fit projects, and (where relevant) a cultural-borrowing risk note.

The SKILL.md Phase 2 question pitches a curated subset (12-15 poles) chosen for variety;
this reference is the full library the agent can pull from when the user describes a
direction not in the curated set, or when the project's character has been pinned in
Phase 1 and a specific lineage is the right call.

Each pole's color values are *starting points*. Refine to fit the project. The hex
choices are documented so the lineage research is recoverable.

## Family A â€” Minimalist / monastic

### Brutally minimal
**Essence:** System fonts, one accent, profound restraint. Every pixel earns its place.
**Palette:** `#000` `#fff` plus one saturated accent (any hue).
**Type:** system-ui stack; one weight; no italics.
**Components:** No shadows, no borders, no radii. Spacing carries all hierarchy.
**Best fit:** Writing tools, single-purpose utilities, hacker products.

### Muji / Hara *kuu* (emptiness)
**Essence:** Not Western minimalism (subtraction toward zero) but *kuu* â€” prior openness
designed to be completed by the user. The product is a vessel, not a statement.
**Palette:** unbleached paper `#F4F0E6`, beige cardboard `#D4C9B5`, light grey `#C9C5BC`,
off-black ink `#222`; never pure white or black.
**Type:** Hara's chosen face was Avenir Next; for mock substitutes use Inter or Atkinson
Hyperlegible at regular weight.
**Components:** Unframed cards, hairline 1px dividers in warm grey, no shadows. Corners
either sharp (0px) or generously round (16px+), never "modern 8px."
**Best fit:** Writing tools, notes apps, content-first products. Distinct from "brutally
minimal" â€” emptiness is *invitation*, restraint is *discipline*.

### Quiet / monastic
**Essence:** Single weight, single color, profound restraint. The page as cell.
**Palette:** `#F8F7F4` paper, `#1A1815` ink, one muted accent for links only.
**Type:** Single serif (Source Serif 4 or EB Garamond), one weight, generous leading.
**Components:** No chrome. Type does everything.
**Best fit:** Long-form reading, contemplative tools, meditation apps.

## Family B â€” Editorial / publication

### Editorial / magazine
**Essence:** Serifs, generous hierarchy, content-as-hero. The print page brought online.
**Palette:** Cream `#F8F5F0`, ink `#1A1A1A`, one editorial color (often a warm red or
navy).
**Type:** Display serif (Fraunces, Sentinel) + body serif (Charter, Tiempos) + small
sans for UI (Inter).
**Components:** Drop caps, pull quotes, marginalia, captioned imagery.
**Best fit:** Substack, longform publications, premium reading products.

### Swiss / International Typographic Style
**Essence:** Mathematical grid + Helvetica/Akzidenz + asymmetric rationalism.
Constructed-systematic, not reductive.
**Palette:** `#000000` `#FFFFFF` `#E53935` (the one accent). That's most of it.
**Type:** Akzidenz-Grotesk Bold + Regular, Helvetica Neue, Univers; tracking
optical-tight.
**Components:** 12-col grid visible at debug; numbered section headers; rotated 90Â°
captions; tabular figures; tight 40-80pt headlines.
**Best fit:** Cultural institutions, architecture practices, design publications,
transit/wayfinding tools.

### Dark academia
**Essence:** Brooding intellectualism â€” leather-bound books, oak panel, candlelight,
Latin marginalia.
**Palette:** `#3A2A1B` `#6B4A2B` `#8B0000` (oxblood) `#C9A227` (gold leaf) `#F5EBDD`
parchment `#1A1410` near-black.
**Type:** Caslon, Garamond, Trajan caps for headers, italicized pull-quotes, marginalia
in Spectral.
**Components:** Drop-cap chapter starts, running-head page numbers, footnote panels,
candle-glow shadows.
**Best fit:** Reading apps, longform publications, literary-criticism tools,
classics-focused publishers.

## Family C â€” Historical movements

### Bauhaus
**Essence:** Primary geometric tokens mapped to primary hues. Shape-as-affordance (per
Itten/Kandinsky: circle=yellow, square=red, triangle=blue).
**Palette:** Red `#D62300`, yellow `#F5C200`, blue `#0050B5`, black, white, 5-step
neutral gray.
**Type:** Universal alphabet (Herbert Bayer) substitute: Futura, Universal Display
Std.
**Components:** Circular = action, square = container, triangular = warning. Strict
geometric primitives.
**Best fit:** Pedagogical products, design tooling, products that want to teach as much
as serve.

### De Stijl
**Essence:** Primary RGB + ortho-only color blocks. Mondrian-as-dashboard.
**Palette:** Pure RGB: `#D40000` `#FFD500` `#0050D4` + black `#000000` on white
`#FFFFFF`. One block of each, never repeated.
**Type:** Geometric sans (Theinhardt, Druk); no italics.
**Components:** "Color block" as the only container; thick black rules between regions;
no diagonals.
**Best fit:** KPI dashboards, severe-modernist brands, products that revel in their grid.

### Russian Constructivism
**Essence:** Diagonal as primary axis. Multi-typeface multi-scale within one headline.
Photomontage.
**Palette:** Vermilion red `#C8102E`, black `#000000`, cream `#F1ECE0`, optional yellow
accent.
**Type:** Slab (Sansita, Ostrich Sans) + grotesque (Inter Black) mixed in one headline.
**Components:** Rotated grid (-15Â° to -45Â°), red wedge dividers, mounted photo blocks,
oversized verbs.
**Best fit:** Manifesto pages, campaign landing, brands that want a *position*, not a
product.

### Memphis Milano (the actual one)
**Essence:** 1981 Sottsass postmodern rebellion â€” squiggles, terrazzo, pastel-and-black,
pattern-on-pattern.
**Palette:** Black-on-pink `#F8C8DC`, teal `#00C2C7`, yellow `#FFE066`, purple `#B967FF`,
red `#FF6B6B`, on white.
**Type:** ITC Officina, Antique Olive, Bauhaus 93 for display; hand-drawn squiggle
accents.
**Components:** Squiggle dividers, terrazzo-pattern fills, geometric shape collages,
black-outlined everything.
**Best fit:** Fashion brands, gen-Z products, music festivals, retro-fashion-aware
brands.

### Dieter Rams / Braun
**Essence:** Refined industrial â€” minimalist with warmth, grid-aligned but not
Swiss-cold.
**Palette:** Warm white `#F4F4F0`, cool gray `#D8D8D8`, charcoal `#333`, signature green
`#00A65A` on actions.
**Type:** Rotis, Avenir; or Inter as a free substitute. Labels in 11px caps.
**Components:** Rotary-dial sliders, segmented controls with chamfered edges, labels in
caps, generous margins.
**Best fit:** Hardware companion apps, tools that want to feel earned, premium products
without flash.

## Risk note: cultural borrowing

Some poles in this catalog (Chromolitho, Korean density, Chinese super-app, BarragĂˇn,
Marimekko, Girih) are tied to specific cultural contexts. When the user picks one of
these poles, the `palette` skill should surface a brief risk-note (see
`cultural-borrowing.md`) and confirm:

- **Borrow the principle**, not the iconography. Generative-tile geometry is
  borrowable; specific girih star patterns from a specific shrine are not.
- **Don't flatten cultures into one flat style.** "Indian Chromolitho" carries
  centuries of religious, regional, and class-specific meaning that varies wildly
  across India.
- **If the project has cultural authority** (built by/for/with the community in
  question), specific iconography may be appropriate. Otherwise, restrict to grammar.

See `cultural-borrowing.md` for the per-pole risk levels and concrete substitution
guidance.
