# Attestation: 2020 Scrum Guide — Product Backlog and Refinement

## Summary

The 2020 Scrum Guide defines the Product Backlog as "an emergent, ordered list of what is needed to improve the product" and "the single source of work undertaken by the Scrum Team." Refinement is treated as an ongoing activity rather than a formal ceremony. The Guide provides no specific cadence, time-box, or guidance on removing or archiving items.

## Key passages

**Product Backlog definition**: "an emergent, ordered list of what is needed to improve the product" and "the single source of work undertaken by the Scrum Team."

**Refinement definition**: "Product Backlog refinement is the act of breaking down and further defining Product Backlog items into smaller more precise items." This is described as an ongoing activity that involves "add[ing] details, such as a description, order, and size," with attributes varying by work domain.

**Readiness criteria**: Items "that can be Done by the Scrum Team within one Sprint are deemed ready for selection in a Sprint Planning event" and "usually acquire this degree of transparency after refining activities."

**Responsibility**: The Developers bear primary responsibility for sizing items. "The Product Owner may influence the Developers by helping them understand and select trade-offs."

**Cadence**: The Guide describes refinement as "an ongoing activity" but provides no specific cadence requirements or time allocation percentages. It is not listed as a formal Scrum event.

**Removal/archival**: The 2020 Scrum Guide contains no guidance on removing or archiving backlog items.
