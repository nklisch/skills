# Attestation: How to Manage Large Product Backlogs — Scrum Alliance

## Summary

A Scrum Alliance article presenting four tactical steps for managing overgrown backlogs: Split, Limit, Eliminate, and Consolidate. Includes eight warning signs of backlog bloat, specific guidance on age limits and zombie item identification, and the distinction between backlog and product discovery systems.

## Key passages

**Warning signs of backlog bloat** (eight indicators):
- Hundreds of items accumulated
- Reluctance to display the backlog
- Difficulty locating specific items
- Development team perceives minimal progress
- Lost track of priorities
- Unable to distinguish refined items from brainstorming ideas
- Chaotic appearance rather than organized structure
- Insufficient time for prioritization activities

**Step 1 — Split**: Separate backlogs when products serve distinctly different user groups or needs.

**Step 2 — Limit**: Three constraint approaches: purpose limit (align to product goal), number limit (cap total open items), and **age limit** (prevent items from becoming stale; remove those exceeding a specified timeframe).

**Step 3 — Eliminate**: "Comb through the backlog, find any item that does not fit, and remove it." Priority targets include **zombie items** (forgotten, irrelevant items added long ago) and duplicates.

**Step 4 — Consolidate**: Merge similar, related, or duplicate items into single entries to reduce clutter.

**Zombie items** defined as: forgotten, irrelevant items that were added long ago and are never prioritized for implementation.

**Hierarchy principle**: Maintain clear structure (themes → epics → user stories → subtasks). Future items require minimal specification since context changes rapidly.

**Granularity balance**: "the higher up in the hierarchy...the less detail needed; the further down...the less detail needed."
