# Attestation: Make the Product Backlog DEEP — Mike Cohn (Mountain Goat Software)

## Summary

Mike Cohn's Mountain Goat Software post independently covers the DEEP framework, collaboratively developed with Roman Pichler. The post emphasizes the backlog as a planning instrument—not a simple task inventory—and directs readers to Cohn's book *Succeeding with Agile* for comprehensive backlog treatment. The post focuses on matching detail level to development timeline and keeping the backlog dynamic.

## Key passages

**Detailed Appropriately**: Stories requiring near-term completion need sufficient clarity for sprint execution, while distant items warrant minimal detail. Guidance emphasizes matching detail level to development timeline.

**Estimated**: The product backlog functions as a planning instrument beyond a simple task inventory. Items closer to implementation receive more precise estimates; those further down the queue have less certain projections.

**Emergent**: Product backlogs remain dynamic rather than static. Items undergo continuous adjustment through addition, removal, and reordering as understanding deepens throughout development cycles.

**Prioritized**: The backlog maintains descending value arrangement, positioning the most valuable work at the top and least valuable at the bottom. This ordering ensures teams consistently maximize delivery value by working sequentially through priorities.

**Refinement guidance**: Cohn states refinement should generate confidence rather than certainty. Directs to the Product Backlog Refinement Guide for nuanced understanding of "sufficient" detail and refinement cessation points.
