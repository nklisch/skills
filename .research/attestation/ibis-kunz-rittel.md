## Summary

This article (Eight to Late, 2009) is a reputable secondary source providing a detailed scholarly
account of Issue-Based Information Systems (IBIS) as originally designed by Horst Rittel and
Werner Kunz in their 1970 working paper "Issues as Elements of Information Systems" (Working
Paper No. 131, Institute of Urban and Regional Development, University of California, Berkeley).
The article attributes the following claims directly to Kunz and Rittel: IBIS was designed to
"support coordination and planning of political decision processes." The system guides
identification, structuring, and settling of issues raised by problem-solving groups. The
article further attributes the concept of "wicked problems" to Rittel's 1973 paper "Dilemmas
in a General Theory of Planning" (co-authored with Melvin Webber), describing IBIS as
specifically designed to handle such problems.

**Cite-through note:** The primary source is Kunz, W. and Rittel, H.W.J. (1970), "Issues as
Elements of Information Systems," Working Paper No. 131, Institute of Urban and Regional
Development, University of California Berkeley. The primary source PDF was not accessible
(ECONNREFUSED) during this fetch session. All Kunz-Rittel claims in the facet brief are
cite-through attributions via this secondary source.
