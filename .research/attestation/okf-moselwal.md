# Attestation: Open Knowledge Format (Moselwal — adoption report)

## Summary

Kai Ole Hartwig / Moselwal Digitalagentur's June 14, 2026 post (2 days after the
announcement), the earliest named adopter found in the survey. Moselwal had a
pre-existing "Moselwal Handbook" (a versioned knowledge base in Markdown + git);
OKF "gave the few conventions a name" so the same files can be consumed by
different agents "without a translation layer." The adoption is real but
low-friction — they were already ~OKF-shaped; OKF was a naming/standardization
event, not a re-architecture.

## Key passages

**The adoption claim (earliest named):**

> We are not discussing OKF from a distance. We have already adopted it in the
> Moselwal Handbook.

**Why it was low-friction for them:**

> the handbook already sat as Markdown in git; OKF gave the few conventions a
> name, so that the same files can in future be consumed by different agents
> without a translation layer

**What the Handbook is:** "the growing, versioned knowledge base with which we
are building Moselwal into an AI company built on agentic engineering." The
handbook is described as publicly viewable.

## Structural metadata

- Author: Kai Ole Hartwig, Moselwal Digitalagentur
- Date: 2026-06-14 (2 days post-announcement)
- The Handbook is publicly viewable (per the post); the adoption is verifiable.
- Adoption shape: low-friction naming/standardization over pre-existing
  Markdown-in-git content — NOT a re-architecture of a substrate with
  provenance/citation machinery (which Moselwal did not have).
